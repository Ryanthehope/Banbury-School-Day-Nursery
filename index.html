Why Parents Choose Us

We know families want reassurance quickly. Our nursery combines warmth, experience and practical year-round support so children can settle confidently and thrive.

Established and trusted

We have been providing childcare in Banbury for over 30 years, building strong relationships with families and the local community.

Rooms by age and stage

Children are cared for in specialist rooms that match their developmental stage, helping them feel secure and well supported.

Year-round practical support

Open from 8:00am to 5:30pm all year round, we help families balance reliable childcare with busy day-to-day life.

Learning through care and play

Every child is supported as an individual through caring relationships, play-based learning and routines that build confidence.

Our Rooms

Each room is designed around the developmental needs of the children in it, staffed by experienced and qualified practitioners.

Star Room

For babies and toddlers aged 9 months to 18 months. A calm, nurturing space focused on attachment and early development.

Moon Room

For children aged 18 months to 2½ years. Focused on language development, independence and creative exploration.

Sun Room

Our pre-school room for children aged 2½ to 5 years. Preparing children for school through structured play and the EYFS curriculum.

A Typical Day At Nursery

Every day balances routines, play and care so children feel secure while building confidence and independence.

1

Arrival and settling in

Children are welcomed into the room, settle with staff and begin the day feeling safe and known.

2

Morning play and activities

Planned activities and child-led play support learning, curiosity and early friendships.

3

Snack and lunch

Mealtimes help build routines, independence and social confidence as part of the nursery day.

4

Outdoor play

Children enjoy fresh air, movement and exploration in our enclosed outdoor spaces.

5

Rest and quiet time

Sleep, quiet activities and calm moments are built around each child’s stage and individual needs.

6

Stories, songs and home time

The day ends with familiar routines, shared moments and handovers to families.

Learning and development

Supporting children through the EYFS

Our curriculum follows the Early Years Foundation Stage, helping children build confidence, communication, physical skills and early understanding through play.

As children grow, we support school readiness with observations, guided activities and early work on letters, sounds and rhyming in pre-school.

Prime areas first

Personal, social and emotional development, communication and language, and physical development.

Growing wider skills

Literacy, mathematics, understanding the world, and expressive arts and design.
