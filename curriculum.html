Curriculum

How we support children’s learning, development and confidence through play every day.

Our Approach

The Early Years Foundation Stage (EYFS) Framework explains how and what your child will be learning to support their healthy development.

Your child will be learning skills, acquiring new knowledge and demonstrating their understanding through seven areas of learning and development.

Areas of Learning

Children should mostly develop three prime areas first. These are:

  • Personal, Social & Emotional Development
  • Communication & Language
  • Physical Development

These prime areas are those most essential for your child’s healthy development and future learning.

As children grow, the prime areas will help them to develop skills in four specific areas. These are:

  • Literacy
  • Mathematics
  • Understanding the World
  • Expressive Arts and Design

Assessment & School Readiness

These seven areas are used to plan your child’s learning and activities. Each child is assessed using the EYFS through a variety of observations made while they are playing.

In the pre-school room, we also work on letters and sounds to encourage phonic and rhyming sounds in preparation for the child moving on to primary school.

At A Glance

  • EYFS-led learning
  • 3 prime areas of learning
  • 4 specific areas of learning
  • Observations used for assessment
  • Letters and sounds in pre-school
